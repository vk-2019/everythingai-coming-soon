AI Generalist Self-Evaluation

  1. 1. Fundamentals: I can explain in my own words what AI, machine learning, and deep learning are, and how they relate to one another.

  2. 2. Math & Statistics: I understand why vectors, matrices, and probability underpin ML algorithms.

  3. 3. Algorithms: I know at least three common ML families (decision trees, neural networks, clustering) and can pick the right one for a given problem.

  4. 4. Data Workflow: I’m confident outlining an end-to-end pipeline: data cleaning, feature engineering, train/test splitting, and EDA.

  5. 5. Model Evaluation: I can choose and interpret metrics (accuracy, F1, RMSE, etc.) and know when to favor precision/recall versus overall accuracy.

  6. 6. Deep Learning & LLMs: I’ve used a neural-network framework (TensorFlow or PyTorch) or can explain how transformers work.

  7. 7. Computer Vision: I understand at a high level how convolutional neural networks process images and why they excel at vision tasks.

  8. 8. MLOps & Deployment: I know the key steps to containerize a model, deploy it as an API, and set up basic monitoring.

  9. 9. Ethics & Governance: I’m aware of common AI fairness and bias pitfalls, and general strategies to mitigate them.

  10. 10. Product Integration: I can describe how to frame an AI feature end-to-end—define the problem, identify data needs, outputs, and success metrics.
